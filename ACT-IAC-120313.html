Want to take an active role in solving government's IT acquisition challenges? Do you have expertise to share? Are you interested in meeting other acquisition professionals?
 
The Acquisition Management (AM) Shared Interest Group (SIG) is forming teams to support 2014 initiatives prioritized by our Government Advisory Panel and we need active volunteers! Industry and government co-leads and team members will have important roles in these key initiatives:
 
 
Refresh the Top 10 Do's and Don'ts in the RFP Process & Continue Efforts Around Mythbusters

Building on the hugely popular 2010 report, the AM SIG plans to update this Top Ten list this winter. What are the new top 10 do's and don'ts for both government and industry? Help plan a forum to identify and tackle these issues; provide your valuable input to shape the AM SIG's FY2014 efforts; and work with OMB to continue busting myths in government-industry communication.
 
 
Leverage Acquisition Training that Promotes Understanding of Government-Industry Processes

Departments and agencies have various training sessions that incorporate industry participants to gain perspectives on processes and concerns. Explore how these training sessions can be leveraged government-wide for greater impact.
 
 
Identify Regulations that Adversely Impact Acquisition

Regulatory requirements can significantly increase time and resources needed by the Government to award and administer acquisitions, as well as increasing the costs of contractor services. Identify regulations that adversely impact the acquisition process and propose regulatory changes that can create efficiencies and achieve savings.
 
 
Industry Day Best Practices

Work with government and industry to explore and document best practices for developing and conducting industry days. This initiative will examine department-wide industry days, procurement-specific industry days and small business conferences.
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
About ACT-IAC Shared Interest Groups (SIGs)
 
SIGs foster collaboration between government and industry professionals to help solve key issues facing our community. Established by the American Council for Technology–Industry Advisory Council (ACT-IAC), the SIGs work to develop an annual work plan of activities and products designed to help solve government-wide issues.
 
About the Acquisition Management (AM) Shared Interest Group (SIG)
 
As a volunteer in the AM SIG you'll work with a team of members to produce white papers, executive reports, best practices, seminars and other products to address federal IT acquisition challenges. The AM SIG addresses acquisition lifecycle management issues, as well as competitive sourcing, acquisition workforce training and procurement and contracting alternatives–focusing on educating federal agencies, Capitol Hill, and regulatory bodies, leveraging the collective experience and expertise of ACT-IAC members, and coordinating the exchange of ideas between government and industry. Our goal is to provide a forum for government and industry to discuss ideas, concepts, and issues related to government acquisition.
